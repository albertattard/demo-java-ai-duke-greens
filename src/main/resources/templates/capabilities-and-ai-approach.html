Our AI approach

  • Start with a measurable business outcome, not a model.
  • Keep product, customer, pricing, policy, and transaction data authoritative in the application or approved enterprise systems.
  • Give AI bounded responsibilities and validate its outputs before business action.
  • Keep a human in control for consequential decisions and state changes.
  • Design for evaluation, monitoring, and iterative improvement.

A concrete example

Duke Greens in practice

AI understands a meal request and suggests meals. The Java application validates ingredients against its curated catalogue. The visitor reviews the basket and explicitly confirms a simulated order.

This keeps AI useful without making it the source of truth for the catalogue, prices, basket contents, or order outcome.
