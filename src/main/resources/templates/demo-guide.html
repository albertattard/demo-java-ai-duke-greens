Duke Greens demo

A one-minute guide

How Duke Greens creates value

Duke Greens demonstrates how a Java application can turn a shopper’s natural-language meal-planning request into suitable meal ideas and a review-only virtual grocery basket.

What you will see

  1. 1

    Tell us what you need

    Describe your household size, dietary preferences, time available, and the kind of meals you want to make.

  2. 2

    AI proposes suitable meals

    AI interprets your request, explains its suggestions, and adapts them when you ask for alternatives.

  3. 3

    The application validates and prepares the basket

    Duke Greens maps the ingredients from the meals you select to its own curated product catalogue and prepares a virtual basket for you to review.

Why this matters

Meal planning can take time and attention. Duke Greens reduces the effort of turning a rough idea into a practical starting point, giving shoppers more relevant recommendations and helping them decide faster.

It also shows a credible path from customer intent to an actionable order: suggestions are connected to products the application can validate, then collected in a basket the shopper can review before taking the next step.

Clear responsibilities

Trust by design

  • AI recommends and explains. It is not the source of truth for products, package sizes, prices, basket contents, or completed orders.
  • The Java application validates recommendations against its own catalogue.
  • You must explicitly confirm the simulated order.
  • This demo does not take payment, arrange delivery, or create a real order.

What to try

Start with one of these requests, then ask for a change if you want to see how the suggestions adapt.

  • “I live alone and need three light vegetarian dinners that I can prepare in 25 minutes after work.”
  • “We are a family of four and need two quick dinners with chicken and plenty of vegetables.”
  • “Suggest three meat-free meals for two people that feel comforting on a cold evening.”
